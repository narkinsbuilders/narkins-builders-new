## The Tax Relief That Changes Everything

The federal government slashed withholding tax (WHT) rates on property purchases from 4% to 2.5% in the highest bracket, with reductions cascading down to 1.5% for lower-value transactions. For a buyer considering **2 bedroom apartments Bahria Town** priced around PKR 45 lakh, this represents savings of over PKR 67,000 in direct tax costs alone.

But the real game-changer came with the complete abolition of the 7% Federal Excise Duty (FED) on property transfers. This single measure removed what had been a punitive burden on buyers of **ready apartments Bahria Town Karachi**, where FED previously added PKR 3.15 lakh to a PKR 45 lakh purchase.

In Islamabad Capital Territory, stamp duty plummeted from 4% to 1%—a 75% reduction that signals the government's commitment to making property transactions less extractive and more accessible.

## Transparency Revolution: Non-Filers Out, Documentation In

The budget's other face is enforcement. Non-filers are now completely barred from purchasing property or vehicles, with advance tax on their cash withdrawals doubled from 0.6% to 1%. Only individuals who submit wealth statements can engage in property deals—a policy designed to flush out undocumented money and protect genuine buyers from speculative bubbles.

Digital monitoring systems using AI now track cement and construction inputs to reduce tax evasion across the supply chain. Budget 2025-26 allocated PKR 5 billion for a markup subsidy scheme in collaboration with the State Bank of Pakistan, specifically targeting low-cost housing finance.
